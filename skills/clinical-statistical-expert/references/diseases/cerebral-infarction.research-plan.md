# Cerebral Infarction Disease Chapter Research Plan

Use this plan to create the first prospective MR-RATE-aligned disease chapter
for the `clinical-statistical-expert` skill.

This is a draft-first plan. It follows the disease research-plan template, but
does not require the full 100-page / 50-image maturity target before producing
an initial reviewable chapter.

## Scope Definition

- Disease or finding: Cerebral infarction.
- MR-RATE label: Cerebral infarction.
- SNOMED CT ID: 432504007.
- SNOMED CT label: Cerebral infarction.
- Original MR-RATE source name: Ischemic infarct.
- Brain-specific scope: ischemic infarction of brain parenchyma, including
  acute, subacute, chronic, territorial, cortical, subcortical, embolic,
  thrombotic, watershed, and small-vessel contexts when source-supported.
- Primary imaging modality: MRI for lesion characterization, especially DWI,
  ADC, FLAIR, T2, T1, SWI/GRE, and post-contrast imaging when relevant.
- Important secondary modalities: noncontrast CT, CTA, CT perfusion, MRA, MR
  perfusion, catheter angiography, vascular ultrasound, PET/SPECT in selected
  contexts, and prior imaging.
- Included variants, phenotypes, stages, or subtypes: acute ischemic infarct,
  subacute infarct, chronic infarct, completed infarct, territorial infarct,
  lacunar infarct, watershed infarct, hemorrhagic transformation, infarct with
  edema or mass effect, infarct with cortical laminar necrosis, and chronic
  encephalomalacia/gliosis as sequelae.
- Excluded related entities: primary intracranial hemorrhage as the main
  diagnosis, cerebral venous sinus thrombosis as a primary disease chapter,
  stroke mimics without infarction, treatment instructions, final patient-level
  diagnosis, and acute triage decisions.
- Intended use: research planning, imaging-review guidance, radiology-report
  language extraction, cohort definition, endpoint design, covariate planning,
  adjudication rules, and MR-RATE label interpretation.
- Explicit non-use: do not use this chapter as a substitute for emergency
  stroke evaluation, treatment eligibility determination, or final clinical
  interpretation.

## Chapter Goals

1. Known-diagnosis characterization goal: describe how cerebral infarction
   appears on MRI and CT across acute, subacute, and chronic stages when the
   diagnosis or suspected ischemic context is known.
2. Differential or mimic-aware review goal: distinguish cerebral infarction
   from hemorrhage, seizure-related signal abnormality, demyelination, tumor,
   infection, migraine, venous infarction, hypoxic-ischemic injury, artifact,
   and chronic gliosis/encephalomalacia when the imaging appearance overlaps.
3. Clinical-statistical translation goal: connect infarct acuity, location,
   territory, size, hemorrhagic transformation, edema, chronicity, and label
   certainty to cohort definitions, endpoints, covariates, adjudication,
   misclassification risk, and claims language.
4. Report-language extraction goal: capture Findings-style and
   Impression-style language for acute restricted diffusion, early CT signs,
   subacute enhancement, chronic encephalomalacia/gliosis, hemorrhagic
   transformation, and uncertainty.
5. Figure or visual-teaching goal: collect link-only starter figures showing
   early CT signs, DWI/ADC restriction, DWI evolution/pseudonormalization,
   perfusion mismatch, subacute infarct/tumor mimic, lacunar infarct, and
   chronic infarct sequelae.

## Source Selection Principle

Use broad authoritative sources for broad claims about cerebral infarction,
imaging workflow, stroke timing, CT/MRI findings, and treatment-context
implications. Use narrow papers only for specific MRI timing, DWI/ADC
evolution, perfusion, or technical measurement claims.

Useful starter sources:

1. ACR Appropriateness Criteria for cerebrovascular diseases, stroke, and
   stroke-related conditions.
2. AHA/ASA 2026 acute ischemic stroke guideline and professional summaries for
   initial imaging, DWI-FLAIR mismatch, vascular imaging, and treatment-window
   context.
3. NICE NG128 and ESO guideline material for additional professional guidance
   and DWI-FLAIR/core-perfusion mismatch context.
4. NCBI Bookshelf/StatPearls stroke imaging and ischemic stroke chapters.
5. Radiology Assistant acute stroke imaging teaching review.
6. PubMed/PubMed Central indexed MRI evolution and acute ischemic stroke
   reviews.
7. PMC Open Access CC BY imaging-method literature for locally reusable figures
   and segmentation endpoint context.
8. Radiopaedia reference articles and cases for specific visual patterns,
   treated as teaching/case evidence rather than broad guideline evidence.

| Ischemic infarct | original source name | MR-RATE SNOMED map | Source synonym for cerebral infarction. | Should map to cerebral infarction unless report context says another organ or non-brain site. |

| Encephalomalacia | sequela label | MR-RATE SNOMED map, NCBI Bookshelf | Chronic tissue loss after prior injury/infarction. | Related to but distinct from active cerebral infarction. |
| Gliosis | sequela label | MR-RATE SNOMED map | Chronic gliotic change around prior injury. | Related but not equivalent to infarction. |
| Lacunar infarct | related MR-RATE label | MR-RATE SNOMED map | Small deep infarct related to small-vessel disease. | Should not be collapsed into all infarcts when phenotype matters. |
| Watershed infarct | related MR-RATE label | MR-RATE SNOMED map | Border-zone infarct pattern. | Often implies hemodynamic or embolic mechanisms needing context. |

## Required Clinical Extraction Questions

- What imaging findings support acute ischemic infarction rather than chronic
  infarct sequelae?
- What does restricted diffusion look like, and is ADC low enough to support
  true restriction rather than T2 shine-through?
- Does the lesion respect an arterial territory, border-zone pattern, or small
  penetrating artery distribution?
- Is there hemorrhagic transformation, edema, mass effect, or vessel occlusion?
- Does prior imaging show new, stable, resolving, or chronic change?
- What findings suggest mimic, mixed process, or need for expert adjudication?

## Required Known-Diagnosis Review Questions

- If the diagnosis is already cerebral infarction, what stage is most likely:
  acute, subacute, chronic, recurrent, or posttreatment?
- Which sequences support acuity: DWI, ADC, FLAIR, T2, SWI/GRE, contrast, or
  perfusion?
- Which features change severity or endpoint definition: territory, size,
  number, location, edema, hemorrhagic transformation, mass effect, vessel
  occlusion, or chronic tissue loss?
- Which findings argue for tumor, demyelination, seizure, infection, venous
  infarct, hypoxic-ischemic injury, migraine, or artifact?

## Required Report-Language Extraction Questions

- How is acute restricted diffusion described in Findings?
- How is cerebral infarction synthesized in Impression?
- Which phrases distinguish acute from subacute, chronic, evolving, completed,
  remote, or old infarct?
- Which report phrases imply uncertainty: "favored infarct", "could represent
  subacute infarct", "tumor not excluded", "recommend follow-up"?
- Which phrases should trigger expert adjudication or sensitivity analysis?

## Required Differential Diagnosis Matrix Extraction

| Comparator | Why it can look similar | Features supporting target disease | Features arguing against target disease | Helpful sequences or context | Report-language cues | Statistical or cohort implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Seizure-related signal abnormality | Can show cortical DWI/FLAIR signal and swelling. | Arterial-territory pattern, persistent infarct evolution, vessel occlusion. | Nonvascular cortical pattern, rapid resolution, seizure history. | EEG, prior/follow-up MRI, perfusion pattern. | "peri-ictal change", "postictal edema" | Exclude or adjudicate if infarct label uncertain. |

| Demyelinating lesion | Can be T2/FLAIR bright and enhance. | Vascular territory and restricted diffusion compatible with infarct. | Ovoid periventricular/callosal pattern, open-ring enhancement, dissemination. | MS history, spine/orbit imaging, CSF, follow-up. | "demyelinating plaque" | Differential label or sensitivity analysis. |
| Hemorrhage | Acute symptoms overlap; hemorrhagic transformation can coexist. | Ischemic territory with DWI/ADC restriction and secondary hemorrhage. | Primary hematoma without ischemic pattern. | NCCT, SWI/GRE, CTA. | "hemorrhagic transformation" | Separate ischemic infarct with hemorrhagic transformation from primary hemorrhage. |
| Chronic gliosis / encephalomalacia | Remote infarct sequelae can be labeled as infarct history. | Prior infarct cavity/volume loss in vascular territory. | No acute DWI restriction or active edema. | Prior imaging, ADC, FLAIR, T1. | "remote infarct", "encephalomalacia" | Separate prevalent chronic label from incident acute infarct endpoint. |

## Required Disease-Course Extraction Questions

- Acute: what does early DWI/ADC restriction look like, and when can CT/FLAIR
  be normal?
- Subacute: when do FLAIR/T2 signal, enhancement, edema, mass effect, and ADC
  pseudonormalization appear?
- Chronic: what indicates encephalomalacia, gliosis, volume loss, ex vacuo
  change, CSF-like cavity, or facilitated diffusion?
- Progression/recurrence: what indicates new infarct, new territory, worsening
  edema, hemorrhagic transformation, or vessel occlusion?
- Improvement/resolution: what active findings resolve and what chronic
  sequelae persist?
