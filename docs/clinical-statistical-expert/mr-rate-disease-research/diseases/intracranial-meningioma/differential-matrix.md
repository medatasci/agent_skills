# Intracranial Meningioma Differential Matrix

Status: final evidence-count target met; needs human expert review.

| Rank | Differential / MR-RATE Contrast | Why It Is Confused | Supports Meningioma | Supports Alternative | Research Handling |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Dural metastasis / brain metastasis | Both can be dural enhancing masses. | Solitary stable dural mass, hyperostosis, calcification. | Cancer history, multiple lesions, bone destruction, rapid change. | Adjudicate dural-based masses. |
| 2 | Solitary fibrous tumor | Dural-based extra-axial tumor. | Typical dural tail/hyperostosis and slow growth. | Lobulated aggressive mass, flow voids, bone erosion. | Separate if favored. |
| 3 | Schwannoma | CPA/skull base enhancing mass. | Broad dural attachment away from nerve canal. | IAC/foramen-centered nerve lesion. | Use location and canal origin. |
| 4 | Glioma | Enhancing mass with edema. | Extra-axial signs, CSF cleft, dural base. | Intra-axial infiltrative FLAIR/callosal spread. | Exclude intra-axial tumors. |
| 5 | Lymphoma | Homogeneous enhancement and restriction. | Extra-axial dural mass with hyperostosis. | Deep/periventricular diffusion restriction. | Adjudicate atypical masses. |
| 6 | Pituitary adenoma | Sellar/parasellar mass. | Dural/skull base origin. | Sellar-centered lesion from pituitary gland. | Location-specific review. |
| 7 | Epidermoid/chordoma | Skull base extra-axial mimics. | Enhancing dural mass. | DWI-bright epidermoid or clival destructive chordoma. | Use DWI/CT bone. |
| 8 | Hyperostosis of skull | Bone change may be dominant. | Enhancing adjacent dural mass. | Isolated bone thickening without soft tissue tumor. | Do not label meningioma from bone alone. |
| 9 | Cerebral edema | Edema may dominate report. | Dural tumor causing vasogenic edema. | Edema from infarct, tumor, PRES, infection. | Use as covariate, not positive label. |
| 10 | Postoperative/radiation change | Enhancement can mimic recurrence. | Nodular growing enhancing residual. | Stable scar or transient enlargement. | Require treatment timing and comparison. |
