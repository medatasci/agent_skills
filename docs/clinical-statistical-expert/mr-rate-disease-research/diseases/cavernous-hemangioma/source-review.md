## What Was Reviewed

- Updated Alliance to Cure Cavernous Malformation consensus guidelines.
- 2017 Angioma Alliance guideline synopsis.
- StatPearls, GeneReviews, NINDS, Mayo Clinic, UCSF, and Radiopaedia
  references for terminology, MRI appearance, genetics, and broad clinical
  framing.
- MRI-focused sources for the classic mixed-signal core, hemosiderin rim,
  GRE/SWI blooming, Zabramski categories, DVA association, and advanced imaging.
- Natural-history evidence for symptomatic hemorrhage, brainstem location,
  prior hemorrhage, seizure endpoints, and untreated follow-up.
- Genetics and mechanism sources for KRIT1/CCM1, CCM2, PDCD10/CCM3, familial
  disease, and mechanistic claim boundaries.
- DVA, pediatric, brainstem, surgical, seizure, radiosurgery, operative video,
  and atypical giant lesion sources.

## What Worked

- The source base strongly supports MRI as the primary modality and supports
  concrete report-language patterns: mixed-signal popcorn lesion, peripheral
  hemosiderin rim, susceptibility blooming, and DVA association.
- Current guidelines provide specific MRI sequence recommendations and useful
  guardrails around genetic testing, follow-up imaging, and angiography.
- Natural-history and treatment-context sources support clinical-statistical
  variables: prior hemorrhage, brainstem location, seizure presentation,
  treatment selection, familial status, and follow-up interval.
- The MR-RATE differential is clear: cerebral hemorrhage, cerebral
  microbleeds, DVA, AVM/fistula, tumor/metastasis, glioma, calcification,
  radiation microbleeds, and traumatic microhemorrhages.

## What Did Not Work

- Several source pages are abstract-only, publisher pages, or intermittently
  browser-challenge accessible, so they are recorded as URL-only evidence.
- No local figures were embedded because reuse rights were not reviewed.
- Videos remain candidate-level because transcripts were not reviewed.

## Claim Boundaries

- Broad imaging appearance claims are supported by guidelines, NCBI sources,
  and radiology reviews.
- Brainstem, pediatric, seizure, radiosurgery, genetic, and operative claims are
  narrower and should remain scoped to those populations or methods.
- Treatment-context material is for research design and cohort interpretation,
  not patient-specific treatment instruction.
