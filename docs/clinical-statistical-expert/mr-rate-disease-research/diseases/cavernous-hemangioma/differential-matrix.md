# Cavernous Hemangioma Differential Matrix

| Comparator | Why It Can Look Similar | Features Supporting Cavernous Malformation | Features Arguing Against Cavernous Malformation | Helpful Sequences Or Context | Example Report Language | Statistical Or Cohort Implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Cerebral hemorrhage | Acute CCM hemorrhage can present as a hematoma. | Persistent mixed-signal lesion, hemosiderin rim, DVA, recurrent same-site hemorrhage. | No underlying lesion after hemorrhage evolves; typical hypertensive ICH pattern. | Follow-up MRI, SWI/GRE, contrast T1, CTA/MRA when vascular lesion suspected. | Hemorrhage centered on a lesion compatible with cavernous malformation. | Separate primary ICH from CCM-related hemorrhage. |
| Silent micro-hemorrhage of brain | Type IV or familial CCM can appear as punctate susceptibility foci. | Larger classic CCMs, family history, genetic variant, DVA or radiation context. | Isolated punctate foci in CAA/hypertension pattern without classic lesions. | SWI/GRE, family history, lesion burden, prior imaging. | Punctate foci may represent small cavernomas in known familial CCM. | Do not pool nonspecific microbleeds with CCM without adjudication. |
| Hemorrhagic metastasis | Blood products, edema, enhancement, and multiplicity overlap. | Popcorn core, rim, stable lesion, DVA, no progressive enhancing mass. | Persistent nodular enhancement, multiple metastasis-like lesions, oncology history. | Contrast MRI, perfusion, spectroscopy, follow-up. | Hemorrhagic neoplasm is less favored but not excluded. | Atypical hemorrhagic masses need exclusion/adjudication. |
| Glioma | Hemorrhagic glioma can have mixed signal and edema. | Well-circumscribed hemosiderin-rimmed lesion without infiltrative expansion. | Infiltrative T2/FLAIR abnormality, progressive mass, elevated perfusion. | Contrast MRI, perfusion, spectroscopy, serial imaging. | Appearance is more compatible with cavernoma than infiltrative neoplasm. | Preserve tumor labels when mass-like behavior persists. |
| AVM or dural AVF | Vascular hemorrhagic lesions can overlap. | Low-flow angiographically occult lesion; no nidus or arterialized drainage. | Flow voids, nidus, early venous drainage, abnormal angiography. | CTA/MRA/DSA when high-flow lesion is suspected. | No high-flow vascular malformation is identified. | AVM/fistula should remain separate vascular labels. |
| Developmental venous anomaly | DVA can be adjacent and conspicuous on contrast MRI. | DVA plus adjacent cavernoma supports sporadic DVA-associated CCM. | DVA alone without hemorrhagic mixed-signal lesion. | Post-contrast T1, SWI/GRE, venous anatomy. | Associated DVA is seen adjacent to the cavernous malformation. | Code DVA association as covariate, not as CCM itself. |
| Calcification | Mineralization can bloom on susceptibility sequences. | Blood-product evolution, mixed core, hemosiderin rim. | Dense stable CT calcification, phase behavior suggesting mineralization. | CT, SWI phase/QSM, stability. | Susceptibility focus may reflect calcification rather than cavernoma. | Uncertain mineralization should trigger sensitivity analysis. |
| Capillary telangiectasia | May be occult or faintly enhancing and susceptibility-visible. | Popcorn lesion, rim, prior hemorrhage, DVA-adjacent cavernoma. | Faint brush-like enhancement, pons location, no mixed blood-product core. | Contrast MRI, SWI/GRE, stability. | Capillary telangiectasia is a consideration for faint enhancement. | Avoid overcalling tiny susceptibility-only lesions as CCM. |
| Radiation-induced microbleeds | Multiple SWI foci after radiation can resemble multiple CCMs. | Larger popcorn lesions, genetic/family context, DVA association. | Radiation field distribution and latency, innumerable punctate foci. | Radiation history, SWI, prior imaging. | Multiple susceptibility foci may be treatment related. | Radiation history is a key covariate/exclusion variable. |
| Traumatic microhemorrhages | DAI microbleeds can mimic punctate CCMs. | Classic larger CCM, DVA, stable/familial pattern. | Gray-white junction/corpus callosum/brainstem trauma pattern. | Trauma history, SWI, distribution, timing. | Distribution favors traumatic microhemorrhages. | Trauma context should prevent false familial CCM labeling. |
