# Lacunar Infarct Differential Matrix

Status: source_backed_draft_complete

Order comparators roughly from most common/practically likely to least common in the relevant MRI reporting context. This draft emphasizes MR-RATE source categories and has been refined against the source-backed lacunar infarct chapter. Expert review should still confirm ordering and report-language nuance.

| Rank | Comparator | MR-RATE category? | Why it can look similar | Findings favoring target | Findings favoring comparator | Helpful modality/sequence/context | Report-language cue | Cohort/statistical implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Cerebral infarction | yes | Lacunar infarct is an ischemic infarct subtype. | Small deep perforator lesion supports lacunar subtype rather than broad territorial infarct. | Large cortical/territorial or embolic/multiterritory pattern supports nonlacunar infarct. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, vascular-territory mapping. | Report may say acute lacunar infarct or small acute infarct in basal ganglia/internal capsule/thalamus/pons. | Represent as subtype plus broad ischemic category when hierarchy is supported. |
| 2 | Watershed infarct | yes | Small deep lesions can be confused with internal border-zone infarcts. | Single small deep perforator territory lesion, rounded/ovoid/tubular morphology. | Linear band or string-of-pearls border-zone pattern parallel to ventricles; carotid stenosis/hypoperfusion context. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, MRA/CTA/carotid imaging. | Reports may mention deep watershed, border-zone, or lacunar pattern. | Subtype distinction changes mechanism covariates and recurrence-risk interpretation. |
| 3 | Silent micro-hemorrhage of brain | yes | Both are small-vessel disease markers and often coexist. | DWI-restricting small infarct or chronic CSF lacune with FLAIR rim. | Punctate susceptibility-only lesion on SWI/GRE without infarct morphology. | SWI/GRE versus DWI/ADC/FLAIR. | Microbleed language should not be mapped to lacunar infarct without infarct evidence. | Microbleeds are covariates/confounders for CSVD burden and bleeding risk. |
| 4 | Gliosis / white matter disease | yes | Small chronic lacunes and WMH/gliosis can be adjacent in CSVD. | Cavitary lacune or acute DWI lesion in perforator territory. | Noncavitary patchy/confluent white matter hyperintensity without lacunar cavity. | FLAIR, T1, DWI, prior imaging. | Report phrases like chronic microvascular ischemic change may not equal lacunar infarct. | Label extraction must distinguish focal lacune from diffuse white matter disease. |
| 5 | Demyelinating disease of central nervous system | yes | Small plaques may occur in periventricular/deep white matter. | Perforator territory location and lacunar morphology. | Periventricular ovoid lesions, Dawson-finger orientation, corpus callosum/juxtacortical lesions, dissemination. | FLAIR, contrast, spine MRI, clinical history. | Uncertain report language should trigger mimic-aware adjudication. | Avoid training labels that collapse demyelinating plaques into lacunes. |
