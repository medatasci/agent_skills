# Silent Micro-Hemorrhage Of Brain Differential Matrix

Status: source-backed draft, needs expansion toward final target

Order comparators roughly from most common or practically likely to less common
in the relevant MRI reporting context. This matrix emphasizes MR-RATE source
categories plus high-yield non-MR-RATE mimics that affect interpretation.

| Rank | Comparator | MR-RATE category? | Why it can look similar | Findings favoring silent micro-hemorrhage | Findings favoring comparator | Helpful modality/sequence/context | Report-language cue | Cohort/statistical implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Cerebral hemorrhage | yes | Both are hemorrhagic findings and may coexist. | Punctate round/ovoid SWI or T2* GRE hypointense foci, usually without edema, mass effect, or CT-visible hematoma. | Macroscopic intraparenchymal hematoma, CT hyperdensity, edema, mass effect, acute/subacute blood-product evolution. | SWI/GRE, CT, T1/T2/FLAIR, serial imaging. | "Punctate chronic microhemorrhages" versus "acute intraparenchymal hemorrhage." | Keep microbleed burden separate from macrohemorrhage endpoints. |
| 2 | Hypertensive microangiopathy / deep small-vessel disease | partial, context for several MR-RATE labels | Deep microbleeds are a marker within this disease pattern. | Deep gray, brainstem, or cerebellar punctate susceptibility foci, often with lacunes and white matter disease. | Hypertensive microangiopathy is the etiologic context rather than a separate lesion; may include lacunes, WMH, deep ICH. | SWI/GRE plus FLAIR/DWI and vascular risk context. | "Deep microbleeds in a hypertensive distribution." | Model etiology/distribution separately from generic microbleed presence. |
| 3 | Cerebral amyloid angiopathy | no direct MR-RATE category, context for lobar microbleeds | Lobar microbleeds are a core imaging marker of CAA. | Strictly lobar/cortical-subcortical microbleeds, posterior tendency, cortical superficial siderosis, older age context. | Mixed deep and lobar pattern argues against pure CAA; other causes require exclusion. | SWI/GRE, Boston criteria context, clinical age/symptoms. | "Lobar-predominant microhemorrhages; consider amyloid angiopathy." | CAA-like distribution may be an analysis subgroup or covariate. |
| 4 | Cavernous hemangioma / cavernous malformation | yes | Small type IV cavernous malformations may be indistinguishable from microbleeds. | Multiple punctate foci without characteristic cavernoma core or DVA, distribution matching CAA/hypertension/trauma. | Popcorn mixed-signal core, hemosiderin rim, developmental venous anomaly, familial cavernoma pattern, larger lesion. | SWI/GRE, T2, contrast MRI, CT calcification, family history. | "Cavernous malformation" should not be collapsed into generic microbleed when characteristic. | Etiology changes natural history and familial implications. |
| 5 | Lacunar infarct | yes | Both are small-vessel disease markers and may be described together. | Susceptibility-only punctate foci without restricted diffusion or CSF-like cavity. | Acute DWI/ADC restriction or chronic CSF-like lacune with FLAIR rim. | SWI/GRE versus DWI/ADC/FLAIR/T1. | "Punctate susceptibility focus" versus "chronic lacunar infarct." | Separate hemorrhagic and ischemic small-vessel markers. |
| 6 | Gliosis / white matter disease | yes | Chronic small-vessel disease reports often mention WMH, gliosis, lacunes, and microbleeds together. | Distinct susceptibility focus on SWI/GRE. | T2/FLAIR hyperintensity without susceptibility lesion; volume loss or gliotic rim. | SWI/GRE and FLAIR comparison. | Do not map "chronic microvascular change" to microbleeds unless susceptibility foci are described. | Avoid label leakage from generic chronic small-vessel disease. |
| 7 | Calcification | no, but common mimic | Calcification can be dark on susceptibility-sensitive images. | Phase/QSM or CT supports blood product; location/distribution fits microbleed pattern. | CT hyperdense calcification, phase/QSM behavior consistent with calcium, common calcification locations. | Noncontrast CT, phase, QSM. | "Calcification versus chronic blood product." | Uncertain foci need exclusion or sensitivity analysis. |
| 8 | Hemorrhagic metastasis | related to brain metastasis | Small hemorrhagic metastases can produce susceptibility foci. | Tiny foci without edema, enhancement, or mass-like lesion, vascular distribution context. | Enhancing lesion, edema, larger or variable-sized hemorrhagic foci, known hemorrhagic malignancy. | Contrast MRI, FLAIR, DWI, prior cancer history. | "Enhancing hemorrhagic lesions concerning for metastases." | Cancer/treatment cohorts need separate tumor-hemorrhage adjudication. |
| 9 | Diffuse axonal injury | no direct category | Traumatic microhemorrhages can be punctate susceptibility foci. | Nontraumatic distribution and small-vessel disease context. | Trauma history, gray-white junction, corpus callosum, dorsolateral brainstem pattern. | SWI/GRE, DWI, trauma timing. | "Punctate traumatic microhemorrhages." | Trauma-related foci should not be pooled with spontaneous small-vessel microbleeds unless intended. |
| 10 | Radiation-induced microbleeds / ARIA-H | no direct category | Treatment-related microhemorrhages can resemble spontaneous CMBs. | No relevant treatment exposure; distribution fits CAA/hypertension. | Prior radiation field or anti-amyloid therapy context; ARIA-H/cSS pattern. | Treatment history, serial SWI/GRE, FLAIR edema. | "Treatment-related microhemorrhages" or "ARIA-H." | Treatment exposure and surveillance timing are essential covariates. |
