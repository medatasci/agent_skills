## What Was Reviewed

- Broad cerebral microbleed review literature for MRI definition, lesion
  morphology, common etiologies, and clinical associations.
- STRIVE-style small-vessel disease standards and vascular brain-health review
  material for nomenclature and research-variable framing.
- SWI/GRE method papers for sequence, field-strength, slice-thickness, echo
  time, and reader-method confounding.
- CAA/Boston criteria sources and radiology references for lobar microbleed
  distribution, cortical superficial siderosis, and mimic context.
- Hypertensive microangiopathy references for deep gray, brainstem, and
  cerebellar distribution contrast.
- Educational video candidates from LearnNeuroradiology, EuroMedicalImaging,
  and stroke/CAA lectures.
- Rating-scale and rater-reliability sources, including MARS and BOMBS.
- Outcome and treatment-context literature for antithrombotic exposure,
  thrombolysis, recurrent stroke/ICH risk, mortality, dementia, and functional
  outcome framing.
- ARIA-H sources for anti-amyloid therapy monitoring and microhemorrhage count
  severity categories.
- Trauma, DAI, automated detection, QSM, and histopathology correlation sources
  for mimic-aware and method-aware claim boundaries.

## What Worked

- The sources agree on the core radiology appearance: small, round or ovoid,
  punctate hypointense foci on T2* GRE or SWI, reflecting hemosiderin from
  prior microscopic hemorrhage.
- The source pack supports practical report-style distinctions between chronic
  microbleeds and acute macrohemorrhage, lacunar infarct, cavernous
  malformation, calcification, hemorrhagic tumor, and traumatic
  microhemorrhage.
- The source pack clearly supports protocol-dependence as a major research
  issue: count and burden can change with SWI versus GRE, field strength, slice
  thickness, echo time, and reader confidence.
- The maturity pass adds useful coverage for standardized rating, burden
  thresholds, ARIA-H, treatment-related risk interpretation, traumatic
  microbleed mimics, and CAA versus hypertensive arteriopathy distribution.

## Claim Boundaries

- Broad imaging appearance and detection-sequence claims are well supported.
- Etiologic claims should remain probabilistic and distribution-based unless
  supported by clinical, pathology, biomarker, or vascular-context evidence.
- Longitudinal progression claims require comparable MRI protocols.
- Treatment and outcome statements should remain research-context framing, not
  patient-specific guidance.
