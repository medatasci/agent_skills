# Intracranial Aneurysm Differential Matrix

Status: final evidence-count target met; needs human expert review.

| Rank | Differential / MR-RATE Contrast | Why It Is Confused | Features Supporting Intracranial Aneurysm | Features Supporting The Alternative | Research Handling |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Infundibulum | Tiny branch-origin outpouching can mimic aneurysm. | Rounded sac with neck, no branch from apex, growth or irregularity. | Funnel-shaped dilation with branch vessel from apex. | Adjudicate tiny outpouchings before cohort inclusion. |
| 2 | Arterial loop or tortuosity | Vessel curve can appear nodular on MRA/CTA. | True dome and neck on multiple planes or DSA. | Continuous vessel without focal sac. | Require vascular imaging confirmation. |
| 3 | AVM / dural AV fistula | Both are vascular lesions and may cause hemorrhage. | Focal arterial sac at branch point. | Nidus, arteriovenous shunting, early draining vein. | Separate vascular malformation labels. |
| 4 | Subarachnoid hemorrhage without visible aneurysm | SAH triggers aneurysm search. | Identified culprit aneurysm or aneurysmal pattern plus vascular lesion. | Perimesencephalic/traumatic/venous/vasculitic pattern with negative angiography. | Do not label aneurysm unless identified or explicitly favored. |
| 5 | Cerebral hemorrhage | Rupture can produce hemorrhage; aneurysm can coexist with parenchymal hematoma. | Vascular outpouching on CTA/MRA/DSA. | Intra-axial hematoma without aneurysm. | Co-label only if both are documented. |
| 6 | Cavernous hemangioma | Hemorrhagic vascular malformation can mimic vascular hemorrhage. | Arterial lumen outpouching and angiographic filling. | Popcorn lesion, hemosiderin rim, DVA, no arterial sac. | Separate lesion class. |
| 7 | Silent micro-hemorrhage of brain | Susceptibility foci can be confused with small vascular lesions in reports. | CTA/MRA/DSA aneurysm sac. | Punctate intra-axial susceptibility foci. | Keep microbleed burden separate. |
| 8 | Hemorrhagic metastasis | Mass-like hemorrhage can mimic thrombosed aneurysm. | Vascular sac, flow void, angiographic filling, arterial connection. | Enhancing intra-axial mass with edema and no arterial neck. | Use contrast MRI/vascular imaging adjudication. |
| 9 | Glioma | Thrombosed giant aneurysm can appear mass-like. | Lamellated thrombus, arterial connection, vascular enhancement. | Infiltrative parenchymal mass, tumor perfusion/spectroscopy. | Require angiographic correlation for mass-like vascular lesions. |
| 10 | Intracranial meningioma | Extra-axial enhancing mass may mimic thrombosed aneurysm. | Flow void/arterial connection and vascular lumen. | Dural tail, broad dural base, homogeneous enhancement. | Use CTA/MRA/DSA when uncertain. |
| 11 | Subdural intracranial hemorrhage | Both can be trauma/hemorrhage contexts. | Aneurysm on vascular imaging; SAH pattern may localize source. | Crescentic extra-axial collection. | Keep SDH separate unless aneurysm is identified. |
| 12 | Cerebral infarction | Aneurysm treatment/vasospasm can cause infarct; MRA ordered for stroke. | Focal arterial outpouching. | Vascular-territory DWI lesion without aneurysm. | Model infarct as complication or separate endpoint. |
| 13 | Cerebral edema | Mass effect from giant aneurysm or hemorrhage can cause edema. | Vascular lesion with lumen/thrombus. | Parenchymal swelling due to tumor, infarct, infection, or trauma. | Use as complication/covariate. |
| 14 | Arachnoid cyst | Incidental MRI context can contain unrelated vascular findings. | Vascular outpouching on angiography. | CSF-intensity extra-axial cyst. | Do not conflate incidental findings. |
| 15 | Treated aneurysm artifact | Clips/coils/stents can obscure residual disease. | Residual or recurrent filling on follow-up vascular imaging. | Artifact without filling or stable treatment construct. | Use treatment-state-specific endpoint definitions. |
