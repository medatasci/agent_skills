# Watershed Infarct Differential Matrix

Status: source_backed_draft_complete

Order comparators roughly from most common/practically likely to least common in the relevant MRI reporting context. This draft emphasizes MR-RATE source categories and has been refined against the source-backed watershed infarct chapter. Expert review should still confirm ordering and mechanism language.

| Rank | Comparator | MR-RATE category? | Why it can look similar | Findings favoring target | Findings favoring comparator | Helpful modality/sequence/context | Report-language cue | Cohort/statistical implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Cerebral infarction | yes | Watershed infarct is a cerebral infarction subtype. | Border-zone distribution at ACA/MCA, MCA/PCA, or internal watershed zones; hypoperfusion/stenosis context. | Single-territory cortical or deep arterial infarct pattern. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, CTA/MRA, perfusion/carotid imaging. | Impression may say watershed/border-zone infarcts or infarcts in a hypoperfusion pattern. | Use hierarchical subtype labeling; do not lose subtype in broad infarct label. |
| 2 | Lacunar infarct | yes | Internal watershed lesions can resemble multiple small deep infarcts. | Linear, band-like, rosary/string-of-pearls border-zone pattern; often multiple and aligned. | Single small perforator lesion in basal ganglia/internal capsule/thalamus/pons. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, vascular imaging. | Reports may distinguish deep watershed from lacunar infarcts by distribution. | Mechanism and covariates differ: hypoperfusion/stenosis versus small vessel disease. |
| 3 | Demyelinating disease of central nervous system | yes | Clinical deficits and white matter lesions can overlap. | Acute DWI lesions in border-zone vascular distribution and vascular stenosis context. | Ovoid periventricular/juxtacortical/callosal lesions, nonvascular distribution, dissemination in time/space. | FLAIR, DWI/ADC, contrast, spine MRI, prior imaging. | Source review notes MS as a watershed mimic; report uncertainty should be captured. | Ambiguous cases should be excluded or adjudicated in endpoint studies. |
| 4 | Gliosis / Encephalomalacia | yes | Chronic watershed infarcts can leave gliosis or encephalomalacia. | Acute/subacute restricted diffusion or interval new lesions. | Chronic volume loss/cystic change/gliosis without acute DWI restriction. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, T1, serial imaging. | Chronic border-zone infarct sequela should be separate from acute watershed infarct endpoint. | Longitudinal status matters for cohort timing and endpoint definitions. |
| 5 | Cerebral edema | yes | Acute infarcts may have edema and mass effect. | Border-zone ischemic lesion pattern drives target diagnosis. | Diffuse edema, tumor edema, infection/inflammation, or nonvascular swelling without infarct pattern. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR/T2, contrast, perfusion, clinical context. | Do not map generic edema alone to watershed infarct. | Edema may be a severity covariate but not a subtype label. |
