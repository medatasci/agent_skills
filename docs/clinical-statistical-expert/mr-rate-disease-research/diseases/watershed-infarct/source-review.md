This cycle expanded watershed infarct from an early review-ready draft into a
target-count evidence package. New coverage focuses on cortical/external and
internal/deep watershed patterns, mixed hemodynamic and embolic mechanisms,
carotid stenosis and collateral status, perfusion and vascular imaging context,
stroke-mimic framing, arterial-territory localization, and video teaching
sources.

## What Was Reviewed

- NCBI MedGen terminology and linked guideline/literature metadata.
- A dedicated watershed-stroke narrative review.
- A Radiographics imaging review of border-zone infarcts.
- A mechanism-oriented MRI ischemic stroke classification review.
- A clinical imaging study of border-zone infarcts.
- ACR cerebrovascular disease appropriateness criteria.
- PubMed indexed review material on border-zone and watershed infarctions.
- Hemodynamic, carotid/collateral, and perfusion sources that support
  mechanism uncertainty and covariate selection.
- General acute-stroke imaging reviews and video teaching sources that support
  workflow, DWI/ADC interpretation, vascular imaging, and perfusion context.

## What Worked

- The source set consistently supports cortical/external and internal/deep
  watershed subtypes.
- The imaging language is distinctive enough for report-style phrasing:
  border-zone distribution, ACA/MCA or MCA/PCA junction, internal watershed,
  centrum semiovale/corona radiata, linear band, rosary-like pattern, and
  string-of-pearls pattern.
- The MR-RATE contrast with lacunar infarct is strong and clinically important.

## What Did Not Work

- Several PMC/PubMed pages opened through browser-challenge pages in this
  environment, so the supporting files record URL-only evidence rather than
  local cached source pages.
- Figure reuse rights were not cleared. Figure candidates remain link-only.
- The evidence-count target is met, but source extraction is uneven.
- Video sources remain candidate evidence until transcript review is complete.

## Evidence Gaps

- Expert review should confirm differential ordering and wording for internal
  watershed versus lacunar infarct.
- Figure candidates remain link-only until reuse rights are reviewed.
- Treatment/outcome context should be reviewed by a stroke-domain expert before
  publication as mature guidance.

## Claim Boundaries

- Broad imaging-pattern claims are supported by multiple broad review/criteria
  sources.
- Mechanism claims should remain probabilistic because watershed infarcts can
  involve hypoperfusion, embolism, or mixed mechanisms.
- Treatment decisions should be framed as clinical pathway context rather than
  patient-level recommendations.
