# Cerebral Hemorrhage Source Review

Date/time: 2026-05-08T01:59:47Z

## Status

Final evidence-count target met; needs human expert review before publication.
The current local package includes 50 source records, 25 figure evidence
records, and 15 video evidence records. Source depth is strong because the
topic is covered by NCBI/Springer imaging textbook-style material, StatPearls,
ACR criteria, AHA/ASA guideline material, Radiopaedia reference articles,
radiology teaching references, and review literature.

## What Was Reviewed

- NCBI Bookshelf/Springer chapter on nontraumatic intracranial hemorrhage.
- StatPearls chapter on intracerebral hemorrhagic stroke.
- ACR cerebrovascular disease appropriateness criteria.
- AHA/ASA 2022 spontaneous intracerebral hemorrhage guideline landing page and
  supporting material.
- Radiopaedia reference articles on intracerebral hemorrhage and hemorrhage on
  MRI.
- PubMed-indexed CT/MRI interpretation review.
- Cerebral amyloid angiopathy/Boston criteria 2.0 sources for lobar hemorrhage
  context.
- Additional review and technical sources on spontaneous ICH imaging, CT/MRI
  blood-product interpretation, SWI/GRE, CTA spot sign, noncontrast CT
  expansion markers, ABC/2 volume measurement, perihematomal edema, outcome
  predictors, and neuroprognostication.
- Video evidence for ICH imaging lectures, hemorrhagic stroke teaching, CT
  blood appearance, and case-based radiology review.

## What Worked

- The NCBI/Springer imaging chapter provides the clearest broad imaging frame:
  CT/CTA initial assessment, MRI etiology characterization, blood-product
  evolution, edema, prognostic factors, and common etiologies.
- The source set supports report-style language for acute hematoma, chronic
  hemosiderin, intraventricular extension, hydrocephalus, edema, mass effect,
  and underlying-lesion workup.
- The MR-RATE contrast with microhemorrhage, subdural hemorrhage, cavernous
  malformation, cerebral infarction, tumor, and cerebral edema is strong.
- The new sources add useful coverage for hemorrhage expansion markers, CTA
  spot sign behavior, CAA-pattern lobar hemorrhage, SWI interpretation, and
  outcome/prognostic variables that affect cohort and endpoint definitions.

## What Did Not Work

- Some PubMed and journal pages are abstract-only or browser-challenge
  accessible in this environment, so they are recorded as URL-only evidence.
- No local figure assets were committed because reuse rights were not reviewed.
- Video records are currently URL-only; transcripts and teaching quality should
  be reviewed before using them as strong evidence.
- Treatment pathway details should be reviewed by a stroke-domain expert before
  calling the chapter mature.

## Evidence Gaps

- Review reuse rights and clinical relevance for figure candidates covering
  chronic hemorrhage, CAA-associated lobar hemorrhage, hemorrhagic
  transformation, and tumor/cavernoma mimics.
- Consider adding a structured table of MRI blood-product stages if future
  users need compact reference behavior.
- Expert review should confirm language around prognostic factors and guideline
  context.

## Claim Boundaries

- Broad imaging appearance and blood-product evolution claims are supported by
  broad sources.
- Etiology should remain probabilistic unless vascular imaging, contrast MRI,
  clinical data, or follow-up confirms the cause.
- Treatment statements should remain pathway context, not patient-level
  recommendations.
