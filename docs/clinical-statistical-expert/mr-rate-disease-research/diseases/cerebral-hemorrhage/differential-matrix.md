# Cerebral hemorrhage Differential Matrix

Status: source_backed_draft_complete

Order comparators roughly from most common/practically likely to least common in the relevant MRI/CT reporting context. This draft emphasizes MR-RATE source categories and has been refined against the source-backed cerebral hemorrhage chapter. Expert review should still confirm etiologic and treatment-context wording.

| Rank | Comparator | MR-RATE category? | Why it can look similar | Findings favoring target | Findings favoring comparator | Helpful modality/sequence/context | Report-language cue | Cohort/statistical implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Cerebral infarction | yes | Stroke presentations overlap; hemorrhagic transformation may complicate infarct. | Primary blood products, hyperdense acute CT blood, SWI/GRE blooming, MRI hemoglobin evolution. | DWI arterial-territory ischemia without primary hemorrhage; vascular occlusion/perfusion pattern. | Noncontrast CT, SWI/GRE, DWI/ADC, serial MRI. | Findings should specify intraparenchymal hemorrhage, hematoma, surrounding edema, mass effect, intraventricular extension if present. | Separate primary hemorrhage, hemorrhagic transformation, and ischemic infarct endpoints. |
| 2 | Silent micro-hemorrhage of brain | yes | Both are hemorrhagic small-vessel/blood-product findings. | Macroscopic parenchymal hematoma with edema/mass effect and acuity. | Punctate susceptibility-only chronic hemosiderin foci, often multiple and clinically silent. | CT for acute hematoma; SWI/GRE for microbleeds. | Do not map punctate microbleeds to cerebral hemorrhage unless report supports macrohemorrhage. | Microbleed burden is a covariate, not usually the same endpoint as acute ICH. |
| 3 | Cavernous hemangioma | yes | Cavernomas may present with hemorrhage and susceptibility. | Large acute hematoma without characteristic cavernoma core/DVA support. | Popcorn/mulberry mixed-signal core, hemosiderin rim, DVA, repeated hemorrhage pattern. | SWI/GRE, T2, contrast MRI, CT for calcification/acute blood. | Impression may say hemorrhage due to cavernous malformation if lesion is visible. | Underlying lesion changes etiology, recurrence, and exclusion criteria. |
| 4 | Subdural intracranial hemorrhage | yes | Both are intracranial hemorrhage categories. | Intra-axial parenchymal location. | Extra-axial crescentic collection along dura, mass effect on cortex, blood/CSF collection planes. | CT, MRI blood-product dating. | Report should distinguish intra-axial from extra-axial hemorrhage. | Location-specific labels are not interchangeable. |
| 5 | Metastatic malignant neoplasm to brain / Glioma | yes | Tumors can bleed and cause edema/mass effect. | Hematoma evolution without enhancing mass after follow-up. | Nodular/ring enhancement, persistent mass, perfusion elevation, multiplicity, edema pattern. | Contrast MRI, follow-up, perfusion. | Report may recommend follow-up MRI to exclude underlying mass. | Need follow-up adjudication when hemorrhage may obscure tumor. |
