# Subdural Intracranial Hemorrhage Differential Matrix

Status: final evidence-count target met; needs human expert review.

| Rank | Differential / MR-RATE Contrast | Why It Is Confused | Features Supporting Subdural Hemorrhage | Features Supporting The Alternative | Research Handling |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Epidural hematoma | Both are extra-axial traumatic blood collections. | Crescentic collection that can cross sutures; bridging-vein/atrophy context. | Lentiform/biconvex collection, often skull fracture, classically suture-limited. | Exclude or adjudicate if report says epidural/extradural. |
| 2 | Subdural hygroma / CSF collection | Chronic SDH can be low density or CSF-like. | Blood-product signal, mixed density, membranes, susceptibility, hyperdense component. | Pure CSF density/intensity, no blood products, no acute-on-chronic language. | Do not count nonspecific hygroma as SDH without blood-product evidence. |
| 3 | Subdural empyema | Crescentic subdural collection can resemble SDH. | Blood products, trauma/anticoagulant context, no diffusion restriction. | Restricted diffusion, rim enhancement, fever, sinusitis/mastoiditis/infection. | Separate infection endpoint and exclude from hemorrhage cohort. |
| 4 | Cerebral hemorrhage | Both are intracranial blood and may coexist after trauma. | Extra-axial crescentic collection compressing cortex. | Intra-axial hematoma centered in brain parenchyma. | Allow co-labels only when both compartments are present. |
| 5 | Traumatic subarachnoid hemorrhage | Trauma often causes both SDH and SAH. | Blood in subdural space along convexity/falx/tentorium. | Blood in sulci, cisterns, fissures. | Keep as separate hemorrhage phenotype. |
| 6 | Cerebral infarction | SDH mass effect or vasospasm can coexist with infarct; symptoms overlap. | Extra-axial blood collection. | Vascular-territory diffusion restriction and parenchymal infarct evolution. | Model as associated complication or separate endpoint. |
| 7 | Cerebral edema | SDH can cause compression and edema; edema can drive symptoms. | Visible extra-axial blood collection. | Parenchymal swelling/signal without subdural blood. | Use edema as covariate or complication, not SDH label alone. |
| 8 | Cerebral atrophy | Atrophy increases SDH risk and creates larger extra-axial spaces. | Blood-product density/signal or mixed collection. | Enlarged sulci/CSF spaces without blood products or mass effect. | Adjust for atrophy; avoid false-positive extra-axial collection labels. |
| 9 | Arachnoid cyst | Extra-axial CSF-intensity lesion can mimic chronic collection. | Crescentic blood-product collection with interval change. | Well-marginated CSF cyst, chronic stable anatomic location, no blood products. | Exclude unless hemorrhage into cyst/subdural space is documented. |
| 10 | Ventriculomegaly / hydrocephalus | Symptoms and imaging mass effect can overlap; shunts can lead to SDH. | Extra-axial subdural blood products. | Ventricular enlargement, transependymal flow, no subdural blood. | Treat shunt/hydrocephalus as covariate when SDH is present. |
| 11 | Intracranial hypotension | Can cause subdural collections and pachymeningeal enhancement. | Blood-product collection if SDH is present. | Brain sag, diffuse pachymeningeal enhancement, venous engorgement, low-pressure context. | Record as etiology/covariate; do not merge with traumatic SDH. |
| 12 | Dural metastasis or pachymeningeal disease | Dural disease can create enhancing extra-axial abnormality or secondary fluid. | Blood-product evolution, crescentic hematoma. | Nodular or diffuse enhancing dural mass/thickening, cancer context. | Adjudicate, especially recurrent or atypical chronic SDH. |
| 13 | Cavernous hemangioma / cavernous malformation | Hemorrhagic lesions can create blood products and susceptibility. | Extra-axial subdural compartment. | Intra-axial popcorn lesion with hemosiderin rim, DVA association. | Separate vascular malformation phenotype. |
| 14 | Silent micro-hemorrhage of brain | Susceptibility artifacts can be confused if only MRI report language is mined. | Extra-axial collection with size/mass effect. | Punctate intra-axial susceptibility foci. | Separate microbleed burden from SDH. |
| 15 | Postoperative extra-axial collection | Postoperative air/fluid/blood can be called collection. | Explicit postoperative subdural blood products or recurrent SDH. | Expected postoperative fluid/air without hematoma language. | Require treatment timing and report-language adjudication. |
