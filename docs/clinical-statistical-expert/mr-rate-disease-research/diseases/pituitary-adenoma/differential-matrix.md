# Pituitary adenoma Differential Matrix

Status: final evidence-count target met; needs human expert review.

| Rank | Differential / MR-RATE Contrast | Why It Is Confused | Supports Pituitary Adenoma | Supports Alternative | Research Handling |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Rathke's pouch cyst | Cystic sellar lesion can mimic cystic adenoma. | Off-midline solid/enhancing component, fluid-fluid level, endocrine adenoma context. | Midline nonenhancing cyst, intracystic nodule, no solid enhancement. | Preserve cystic versus solid label. |
| 2 | Empty sella syndrome | Sellar expansion and flattened gland can be mistaken for prior or cystic mass. | Discrete enhancing or hypoenhancing mass. | CSF-filled sella with flattened gland and no solid tumor. | Exclude anatomic variant. |
| 3 | Intracranial meningioma | Tuberculum sellae/cavernous sinus meningioma can mimic macroadenoma. | Intrasellar origin, gland involvement, delayed enhancement, endocrine phenotype. | Dural base, dural tail, hyperostosis, separate pituitary. | Use dural and bone signs. |
| 4 | Craniopharyngioma / cystic sellar mimic | Suprasellar cystic/solid mass overlaps. | Sellar origin and adenoma enhancement pattern. | Calcification, suprasellar epicenter, adamantinomatous/papillary pattern. | Use CT and location. |
| 5 | Intracranial aneurysm | Round sellar/parasellar lesion can mimic mass. | Enhancing soft tissue mass without flow void. | Flow void, vascular enhancement, CTA/MRA continuity. | Require vascular imaging when uncertain. |
| 6 | Hypophysitis | Enlarged enhancing pituitary and stalk can mimic adenoma. | Focal adenoma, delayed enhancement, macroadenoma mass effect. | Symmetric gland/stalk thickening, inflammatory or immune-therapy context. | Ask for treatment and systemic context. |
| 7 | Metastatic malignant neoplasm to brain | Pituitary metastasis can cause sellar mass and diabetes insipidus. | Indolent adenoma pattern and endocrine adenoma phenotype. | Cancer history, stalk/posterior pituitary involvement, rapid growth, DI. | Preserve systemic cancer context. |
| 8 | Schwannoma | Cavernous sinus/parasellar mass overlap. | Sellar pituitary origin. | Nerve-centered lesion along trigeminal or other cranial nerve. | Preserve location and nerve of origin. |
| 9 | Lipoma of brain | Fat-containing sellar/parasellar lesion may be called mass. | Enhancing adenoma tissue. | T1-bright fat signal and fat suppression. | Use fat-suppressed imaging. |
| 10 | Arachnoid cyst | CSF-intensity suprasellar/sellar lesion. | Solid or hypoenhancing pituitary mass. | CSF signal, no enhancement, no restriction. | Classify separately. |
| 11 | Glioma / optic pathway lesion | Suprasellar optic pathway mass may be near pituitary. | Sellar origin and pituitary endocrine context. | Intra-axial optic pathway/hypothalamic origin. | Use compartment signs. |
| 12 | Postoperative change | Packing material and enhancement mimic residual tumor. | Nodular growing enhancement. | Thin peripheral enhancement and expected postoperative change. | Require postoperative baseline and interval follow-up. |
