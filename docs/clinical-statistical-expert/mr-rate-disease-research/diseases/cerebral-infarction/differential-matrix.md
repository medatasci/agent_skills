# Cerebral Infarction Differential Matrix

Status: first_pass_source_discovery_started

Order comparators roughly from most common/practically likely to least common in the relevant MRI reporting context. This first pass emphasizes MR-RATE source categories and must be refined after deeper source review.

| Rank | Comparator | MR-RATE category? | Why it can look similar | Findings favoring target | Findings favoring comparator | Helpful modality/sequence/context | Report-language cue | Cohort/statistical implication |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Lacunar infarct | yes | Both are ischemic infarcts and may be acute on DWI. | Cortical/territorial, wedge-shaped, vascular-territory pattern, larger lesion, cortical signs, vessel occlusion/perfusion deficit. | Small deep perforator territory lesion, rounded/ovoid/tubular morphology, basal ganglia/internal capsule/thalamus/pons distribution. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, vascular imaging, perfusion, lesion distribution. | Findings may say acute/subacute infarct in a vascular territory; impression synthesizes artery/territory and acuity. | Subtype misclassification changes endpoint grouping and mechanism-specific covariates. |
| 2 | Watershed infarct | yes | Watershed is a subtype of ischemic infarct and can be included under broad cerebral infarction labels. | Classic territorial infarct conforms to one arterial supply rather than border-zone junctions. | Cortical ACA/MCA or MCA/PCA border-zone lesions, deep internal border-zone band/string-of-pearls pattern, hypoperfusion/stenosis context. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, CTA/MRA/carotid evaluation, perfusion. | Report language may mention border-zone, watershed, or hypoperfusion pattern. | Need hierarchical labels: broad infarct positive plus subtype watershed when source supports it. |
| 3 | Cerebral hemorrhage | yes | Acute neurologic deficit and edema/mass effect can overlap clinically and on CT workups. | Restricted diffusion in arterial territory without susceptibility/blood products as primary finding. | T1/T2/SWI/GRE blood-product evolution, susceptibility blooming, hyperdense acute hemorrhage on CT. | SWI/GRE, noncontrast CT, DWI/ADC. | Impression should not collapse hemorrhagic transformation into primary hemorrhage without support. | Hemorrhage exclusion and hemorrhagic transformation status are major cohort covariates. |
| 4 | Demyelinating disease of central nervous system | yes | Acute demyelinating plaques may restrict diffusion or enhance and mimic infarct. | Vascular-territory distribution, DWI/ADC evolution, vascular occlusion/perfusion context. | Ovoid periventricular/juxtacortical/corpus callosal lesions, dissemination in space/time, open-ring enhancement. | FLAIR, DWI/ADC, contrast, spine MRI, prior imaging. | Reports may hedge: demyelinating plaque versus subacute infarct. | Ambiguous lesions need adjudication/sensitivity analysis. |
| 5 | Glioma or metastatic malignant neoplasm to brain | yes | Mass-like infarct, subacute enhancement, edema, or diffusion abnormality can mimic tumor. | Arterial territory, temporal evolution toward encephalomalacia/gliosis, vascular cause. | Mass effect out of proportion, nodular/ring enhancement, progressive growth, perfusion elevation, multiplicity with edema. | Contrast MRI, perfusion, spectroscopy, serial imaging. | Impression may recommend follow-up to exclude neoplasm when atypical. | Tumor mimics affect exclusion criteria and follow-up endpoints. |
| 6 | Gliosis / Encephalomalacia | yes | Chronic infarct becomes gliosis/encephalomalacia and may be reported as sequela. | Acute/subacute diffusion restriction and vascular-territory acuity. | Volume loss, cystic encephalomalacia, chronic FLAIR gliosis, no acute restricted diffusion. | DWI/ADC, FLAIR, T1, prior imaging. | Impression may say chronic infarct/encephalomalacia rather than acute infarct. | Separate incident acute infarct endpoints from chronic sequelae labels. |
