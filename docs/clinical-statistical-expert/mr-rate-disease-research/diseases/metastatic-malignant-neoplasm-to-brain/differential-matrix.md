# Metastatic Malignant Neoplasm To Brain Differential Matrix

Status: final evidence-count target met; needs human expert review.

| Rank | Differential / MR-RATE Contrast | Why It Is Confused | Features Supporting Brain Metastasis | Features Supporting The Alternative | Research Handling |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 1 | Glioma / glioblastoma | Solitary necrotic enhancing mass can mimic metastasis. | Multiple gray-white junction lesions, known systemic cancer, circumscribed edema pattern. | Infiltrative FLAIR, corpus callosum crossing, cortical/subcortical invasion. | Adjudicate solitary lesions; do not rely on enhancement alone. |
| 2 | Brain abscess | Ring-enhancing lesions overlap. | Known cancer, multiple lesions, no marked central diffusion restriction. | Central diffusion restriction, infection context, capsule pattern. | Exclude or adjudicate infection-like lesions. |
| 3 | Radiation necrosis / pseudoprogression | Treated metastases can enlarge and enhance. | Rising perfusion, serial growth, new lesions, viable tumor context. | Low perfusion, treatment-field location, delayed radiation injury, spontaneous stabilization. | Separate progression from treatment effect endpoints. |
| 4 | Primary CNS lymphoma | Enhancing mass and diffusion restriction overlap. | Multiple cortical/subcortical metastases with edema and known primary. | Deep periventricular homogeneous enhancement and marked restriction. | Adjudicate especially in immunocompromised patients. |
| 5 | Tumefactive demyelination | Ring enhancement and edema overlap. | Cancer history, multiple metastatic distribution, nodular/ring enhancement. | Open-ring enhancement, MS-like lesions, less mass effect than expected. | Require demyelination review for uncertain solitary lesions. |
| 6 | Dural metastasis versus meningioma | Dural enhancing masses overlap. | Known systemic malignancy, multiple dural lesions, bone destruction, rapid change. | Classic meningioma features, hyperostosis, stable dural tail mass. | Keep dural metastasis separate from parenchymal BM when possible. |
| 7 | Cerebral hemorrhage | Hemorrhagic metastases can present as hematoma. | Enhancing mass, multiple lesions, cancer context. | Typical hypertensive/lobar hemorrhage without enhancing mass. | Co-label only when both hemorrhage and metastasis are supported. |
| 8 | Cavernous hemangioma | Hemorrhagic lesion with susceptibility can mimic metastasis. | Enhancing mass and edema, systemic cancer context. | Popcorn lesion, hemosiderin rim, DVA, stable chronic lesion. | Separate vascular malformation phenotype. |
| 9 | Cerebral edema | Edema may be the dominant report finding. | Enhancing tumor focus causing vasogenic edema. | Edema from infarct, infection, trauma, PRES, or treatment. | Use edema as covariate/complication, not positive BM label alone. |
| 10 | Cerebral infarction | Subacute infarct can enhance and cause edema. | Round enhancing mass not vascular-territory, known cancer metastasis pattern. | Vascular-territory DWI/ADC evolution. | Require vascular-territory review for new enhancing lesions. |
| 11 | Intracranial meningioma | Dural-based metastasis and meningioma overlap. | Multiple dural masses, destructive bone, rapid progression, systemic cancer. | Stable extra-axial mass, calcification/hyperostosis, classic dural tail. | Do not count meningioma as metastasis unless report says dural metastasis. |
| 12 | Arachnoid cyst | Incidental cystic lesion in oncology imaging. | Enhancing wall/nodule and edema. | CSF intensity, no enhancement, chronic stability. | Exclude incidental benign cysts. |
| 13 | Cerebral microbleeds | Hemorrhagic metastases and susceptibility foci overlap on SWI. | Enhancing hemorrhagic lesion, edema, mass effect. | Punctate susceptibility without enhancing mass. | Keep microbleed burden separate. |
| 14 | Leptomeningeal disease | May be described without focal parenchymal metastasis. | Nodular/sulcal/cisternal enhancement plus known cancer. | Infection/inflammation or postoperative enhancement. | Store as subtype when possible. |
| 15 | Postoperative or post-radiation change | Enhancement at treated site can mimic recurrence. | Nodular enlarging enhancement, high perfusion, progressive serial change. | Expected timing/location, low perfusion, stable or resolving enhancement. | Require treatment date and serial imaging. |
