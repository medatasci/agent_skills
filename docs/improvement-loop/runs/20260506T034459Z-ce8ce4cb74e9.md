## What Went Well

- The new score is fully deterministic and derived from the existing source-context map rather than introducing an LLM-only judgment.
- The test fixture already contained seven of the eight artifact categories, which made it a good regression check for both present and missing coverage.
- Documentation now states the boundary clearly: coverage means evidence breadth was detected, not that the upstream tool is safe, runnable, clinically valid, or ready to publish.

## What Could Be Improved

- The coverage score is still coarse. It answers "what kinds of evidence were found?" but not "is the evidence sufficient or trustworthy?"
- The current candidate table can become wide as more review columns are added. A later report format may need per-candidate detail cards or collapsible sections.
- The next improvement should connect coverage to concrete reviewer actions, such as "find missing model card" or "verify license file" instead of only listing missing categories.
