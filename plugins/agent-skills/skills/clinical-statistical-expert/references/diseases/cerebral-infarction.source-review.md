# Cerebral Infarction Source Review

Status: expanded source review for a first draft.

This review supports `cerebral-infarction.md`. The source pack now identifies
well over 100 pages of relevant material across professional guidelines,
textbook-style chapters, radiology teaching, broad reviews, and open-access
imaging-method articles. It is not yet mature enough to call the disease
chapter complete because the sources still need deeper extraction and clinical
expert adjudication.

## What The Sources Support

- Noncontrast CT is central in acute stroke evaluation because it rapidly helps
  exclude hemorrhage and can show early ischemic signs.
- MRI, especially DWI with ADC maps, is highly useful for acute infarct
  detection and lesion characterization.
- FLAIR/T2 and CT abnormalities can lag behind DWI/ADC early in the course.
- ADC pseudonormalization and persistent T2/FLAIR signal create important
  subacute timing pitfalls.
- Chronic infarcts may leave volume loss, encephalomalacia, CSF-like cavities,
  ex vacuo change, and gliosis.
- Vascular imaging and perfusion imaging matter when vessel occlusion, core,
  penumbra, or treatment-context questions are in scope.
- DWI lesion volume, manual annotation, automated segmentation, and final
  infarct volume are different research endpoints that need explicit mapping.
- Acute ischemic lesion examples can be embedded locally when sourced from CC BY
  open-access material with attribution.

## What The Sources Do Not Yet Fully Support

- A complete stroke-mimic chapter-level review.
- A fully extracted outcomes and prognostic-factor section from the 2026
  AHA/ASA guideline and broader outcome literature.
- Mature lacunar, watershed, posterior fossa, and hemorrhagic transformation
  subsections.
- A full set of realistic report-language examples drawn from multiple source
  cases or local MR-RATE reports.
- Figure evidence maturity beyond the two local CC BY figures now added.

## Drafting Guidance

Write the chapter as a source-backed draft. Prefer concrete imaging language:

- "punctate or territorial focus of high DWI signal with corresponding low ADC"
- "wedge-shaped cortical/subcortical abnormality in an arterial distribution"
- "loss of gray-white differentiation and sulcal effacement"
- "gyriform enhancement in the subacute phase"
- "CSF-like encephalomalacic cavity with surrounding FLAIR hyperintense gliosis"

Keep treatment discussion at research-context level. Do not write patient-level
treatment instructions.
